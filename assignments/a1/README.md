# Assignment 1: Quantum Mechanics Foundations

## Overview

This assignment covers the fundamental mathematical and physical principles underlying quantum computing, including linear algebra, quantum states, measurement theory, and density matrices.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Linear Algebra & Vector Spaces
- **Complex Vector Spaces**: Working with complex Hilbert spaces
- **Inner Products**: Bra-ket notation and orthonormal bases
- **Tensor Products**: Composite quantum systems and multi-qubit states
- **Matrix Representations**: Linear operators and observables

### 2. Quantum States
- **Pure States**: State vectors and normalization
- **Superposition Principle**: Linear combinations of basis states
- **Bloch Sphere**: Geometric representation of single-qubit states
- **Multi-qubit Systems**: Entangled vs. separable states

### 3. Quantum Measurement
- **Measurement Postulate**: Collapse and Born rule
- **Observables**: Hermitian operators and eigenvalue decomposition
- **Projective Measurements**: Projection operators and measurement outcomes
- **Expectation Values**: Computing average measurement results

### 4. Density Matrices
- **Mixed States**: Statistical ensembles and classical uncertainty
- **Density Operator Formalism**: Trace, positivity, and unit trace properties
- **Partial Trace**: Reduced density matrices for subsystems
- **Entanglement Detection**: Separability criteria and purity measures

## Key Concepts

- **Dirac Notation**: Bra-ket formalism for quantum states
- **Eigenvalue Problems**: Solving for measurement outcomes
- **Trace Operations**: Computing probabilities from density matrices
- **Schmidt Decomposition**: Canonical form for bipartite states

## Mathematical Tools

- Matrix diagonalization
- Tensor product calculations
- Trace computations
- Inner product evaluations
- Probability distribution derivations

## Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, students should be able to:
1. Represent quantum states using vectors and density matrices
2. Compute measurement probabilities using the Born rule
3. Perform tensor product calculations for multi-qubit systems
4. Analyze quantum states using density matrix formalism
5. Distinguish between pure and mixed states
6. Calculate expectation values of observables
